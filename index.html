// About

I am a researcher at the intersection of scientific machine learning and space physics, currently collaborating with ISRO on detecting solar wind switchbacks from in-situ magnetic field and plasma time-series data of NASA's Parker Solar Probe. My work spans end-to-end signal processing pipelines, deep sequence models (1D-CNN, BiLSTM, Transformers), and rigorous evaluation against expert-labelled intervals.

Previously I completed an MSc in Data Science (CGPA 8.8/10) at Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham, with a thesis on multi-objective company performance scoring using Adaptive Discriminant Analysis and KMeans clustering. I have published in IEEE and Springer Nature, and I am currently seeking PhD opportunities in machine learning for space weather and heliophysics.

// Research

Solar Wind Switchback Detection — NASA Parker Solar Probe

Mar 2026 — Present

Building and evaluating 1D-CNN, BiLSTM, and Transformer-based classification models on in-situ magnetic field and plasma time-series data from NASA's Parker Solar Probe (PSP). Developing end-to-end signal processing pipelines including noise filtering, feature extraction, and temporal segmentation. Model predictions are evaluated against expert-labelled switchback intervals with iterative refinement cycles. Paper in preparation.
